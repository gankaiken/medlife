# Executive Brief

## Problem
Medical educators need safe, traceable, low-friction simulation practice for early clinical reasoning, communication, and patient safety without overstating automation as formal assessment.

## Proposed Solution
Medlife is a learner-facing consultation simulator with guided and AI-patient modes, explicit evidence capture, versioned cases, formative debriefing, and pilot review workflows.

## Intended Students
- Candidate early-clinical Monash University Malaysia medical students
- Especially Year 2 to Year 3 transition and early core-clinical learners

## Intended Educational Use
- Formative practice
- Pre-clinical to clinical transition rehearsal
- Small-group or individual supervised pilot use

## What Is Working
- Learner flow, persistence, AI fallback, and browser reliability
- Case governance, export, and reviewable evidence traces
- Controlled educator/reviewer workflow foundations

## What Is Not Yet Validated
- Official Monash curriculum approval
- Formal assessment validity
- Clinical certification
- Institution-scale deployment or integration

## Pilot Request
Run a controlled faculty-reviewed pilot with defined learners, reviewed cases, educator sampling, and explicit consent boundaries.

## Faculty Involvement Required
- Academic sponsor
- Clinical reviewers
- Curriculum reviewers
- Assessment and simulation educators

## Risks
- Clinical inaccuracy
- Overreliance on automation
- Accessibility gaps
- Privacy and governance concerns

## Success Criteria
- Students can complete cases and reflections
- Educators can review encounters and compare judgement
- Technical failure rate remains low
- Pilot data is usable for improvement without overstating validity
