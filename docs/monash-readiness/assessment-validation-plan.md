# Assessment Validation Plan

## Purpose
Compare Medlife automated formative feedback with educator judgement during a controlled pilot.

## Proposed Workflow
1. Select a balanced sample of encounters across the active pilot cases, learner stages, and difficulty levels.
2. Blind educators to the automated formative verdict before they submit independent ratings wherever the pilot design allows.
3. Capture rubric version, overall category or score, domain scores, safety findings, missed-history concepts, confidence, and review time for each educator rating.
4. Record separate educator agreement or disagreement comments after the independent score is locked.
5. Preserve later amendments as new audit entries instead of overwriting the original judgement.
6. Review disagreements through a documented adjudication pass led by authorised educators, not by Medlife automation.

## Candidate Metrics
- Percentage agreement
- Weighted agreement where categories are ordered
- Domain-level agreement
- Safety-flag confusion matrix
- Inter-rater agreement between educators
- Correlation as an exploratory measure only

## Proposed Sample
- Initial target: at least 24 to 36 independently educator-rated attempts before drawing any comparison conclusions.
- Minimum reporting threshold: sample size displayed prominently, with a small-sample warning below 8 reviewed attempts.
- Multiple educators may rate the same attempt to support inter-rater analysis.

## Rating Procedure
- Use the same rubric version for the same comparison batch.
- Keep automated and educator scores visually separate.
- Do not prepopulate the independent educator form from Medlife automated output.
- Label test-fixture reviews clearly and exclude them from production validity claims.

## Blinding Approach
- Preferred mode: educators review transcript, learner actions, and evidence before seeing Medlife's automated debrief.
- Fallback mode: if the pilot requires combined review, mark the score as non-independent or assisted.

## Disagreement Adjudication
- Safety-critical disagreements are escalated for explicit educator discussion.
- Curriculum or clinical approval decisions remain independent from agreement statistics.
- Agreement figures are descriptive only and do not grant assessment validation by themselves.

## Important Limits
- One metric does not prove validity.
- Small pilot samples must not be overinterpreted.
- AI-assisted feedback remains formative and subordinate to human review.
- Correlation must not be described as agreement.
- High agreement must not be described as educational validity, curriculum approval, or formal assessment validation.

## Data Needed
- Encounter transcript
- Learner actions
- Debrief domain scores
- Safety findings
- Educator review comments
- Educator agreement/disagreement labels
